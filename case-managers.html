Case Managers

What is Targeted Case Management (TCM)?

Targeted case management is a service that provides extra support for a client who needs a little more guidance in finding appropriate services and agencies throughout their community to meet their needs. TCMs advocate for clients to ensure that their needs are being adequately met.

What are some things that TCMs can help with? 

Here are just a few of the things TCMs can help client navigate. 

  • Applying for SNAP benefits
  • Linking clients to food pantries
  • Helping find a Primary Care Provider or medical services
  • Assisting with IEP or school needs
  • Assisting with finding appropriate housing/housing services
  • Referring to community agencies that assist with household utilities
  • Helping with resume building and employment opportunities
  • TCMs can assist with finding childcare
  • Referring to community activities and events to promote self esteem
  • Helping find support groups
  • And so much more

What are the requirements for TCM?

For someone to be approved for TCM, they must 4 years old and older and receive Medicaid, currently seeing a therapist and have a qualifying diagnosis.


TCM is not meant to be a long term service, but a short term to help clients get linked to appropriate services/community partners or agencies.


If you think you might be interested in TCM services, let your therapist know.
